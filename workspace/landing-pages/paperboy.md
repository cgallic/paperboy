# Landing Page Copy: Paperboy

## Meta

- **Target audience:** Technical founders and hands-on product/engineering leads
- **Traffic source:** Cold paid traffic
- **Goal:** Request a free personalized sample brief
- **Offer after value:** Optional $49/month founding pilot
- **Evidence tier:** Heuristic rewrite pending real traffic and buyer interviews

## Wireframe

1. Hero: name the manual behavior and offer the free sample
2. Example: show the structure and specificity of one brief item
3. How it works: request, sources, sample, then purchase decision
4. Access boundary: no Gmail access and public, read-only GitHub context
5. Pricing: free sample first, $49/month only after the reader sees it
6. FAQ: answer access, automation, input, and payment objections
7. Final CTA: repeat the free personalized sample

## Winning Copy

### Hero

**Headline:** Stop reading 20 newsletters every morning.

**Subhead:** Forward the newsletters you trust, choose the public GitHub repos you follow, and get one daily brief with what changed and why it matters.

**CTA:** Get my free sample brief

**Risk reversal:** No Gmail access. No credit card. Your first sample is free.

### Example

**Headline:** One short brief. Only changes worth your attention.

Each item says what changed, why it matters, and what to check next. If nothing matters, Paperboy says so.

### How It Works

1. Request your free sample.
2. Send a few newsletters or links and name the public repos you follow.
3. We build your sample and link every source.
4. Keep the daily version for $49/month only if the sample earns a place in your morning.

### Access Boundary

**Headline:** You choose what Paperboy reads.

Paperboy never connects to Gmail. Forward only the messages you choose. Public GitHub context is read-only, and Paperboy cannot change your code.

### Pricing

**Headline:** Your first personalized brief is free.

If it earns a place in your morning, the founding pilot is $49/month. If it does not, do not continue.

### Final CTA

**Headline:** Find out what Paperboy would send you tomorrow.

**CTA:** Get my free sample brief

## Quality Gate Results

- **Four U's:** 13/16 — Unique 3, Useful 4, Ultra-specific 4, Urgent 2
- **Perception engineering:** 8/9 — manual reading is reframed as unnecessary work; the free sample makes the decision a test; no-card access removes risk
- **Banned words:** Pass
- **AI slop:** Pass
- **Single primary CTA:** Pass
- **Claims:** No customer, revenue, time-saving, or automation proof claimed
- **First test:** Headline and free-sample CTA against the current baseline; primary metric is persisted sample requests per eligible visit
